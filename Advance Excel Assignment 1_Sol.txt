2. How can you restrict someone from copying a cell from your worksheet?

Ans: In Excel, you can restrict someone from copying a cell or a range of cells from your worksheet by applying protection to the worksheet:

     1.Select the cell or range of cells that you want to protect from being copied.
     2.Right-click on the selected cells and choose "Format Cells" from the context menu.
     3.In the Format Cells dialog box, go to the Protection tab.
     4.Uncheck the "Locked" checkbox. Then by default, all cells are locked in Excel.
     5.Click OK to close the Format Cells dialog box.
     6.Then go to the Review tab on the Excel ribbon.Click on the "Protect Sheet" option.
     7.In the Protect Sheet dialog box, you can set a password to prevent others from unprotecting the sheet without the password.Alternatively, 
       you can leave the password field blank if you don't want to set a password.
     8.Select the specific actions you want to allow users to perform, such as selecting locked cells, formatting cells, 
       or inserting/deleting columns and rows.
     9.Ensure that the "Protect worksheet and contents of locked cells" option is checked.Then click OK to apply the protection.

5. What are the things that we can notice after opening the Excel interface?

Ans: After opening the Excel interface, there are several things that you may notice. Here are some key elements:

 1. Workbook: Excel opens with a blank workbook, which is the main file where you can enter and organize your data.
 2. Ribbon: The Ribbon is a horizontal strip located at the top of the Excel window. It contains tabs, such as Home, Insert, Formulas, etc.
 3. Worksheet: A worksheet is a grid of cells where you can enter and manipulate data. By default, a new workbook opens with a single worksheet
    named Sheet1.
 4. Cells: The grid of cells is the main area where you enter and display data.
 5. Formula Bar: The Formula Bar is located just above the worksheet grid. It displays the contents of the currently selected cell.
 6. Name Box: The Name Box is located next to the Formula Bar and displays the cell reference or name of the currently selected cell or range.
 7. Status Bar: The Status Bar is located at the bottom of the Excel window. It provides information about the current state of the worksheet, 
    such as the sum or count of selected cells.

6. When to use a relative cell reference in excel?

Ans: A relative cell reference is used in Excel when you want a formula or function to adjust its cell references based on the relative
     position of the formula when copied or filled to other cells. Relative cell references are the default type of reference in Excel.

      b.Calculations that involve adjacent cells.
